Getting around safely

Taxis

📱 Use the Beat app

Beat (Greece's main ride-hailing app, like Uber) works in Athens, Santorini and Mykonos. Download it before you go — it shows the price upfront and you don't need to negotiate or speak Greek.

☎️ Local taxi ranks

Island taxi fleets are small and phone numbers change season to season — ask your hostel front desk for the current local taxi rank number when you check in, especially late at night when apps may have long waits.

Before you fly

Consular help & prep

🇺🇸 U.S. Embassy Athens

Enroll in the free STEP program (step.state.gov) before you leave — it registers your trip with the State Department and sends safety alerts. Save the Embassy's after-hours emergency contact from gr.usembassy.gov to your phone before departure.

🧳 Trip prep checklist

  • Photo of everyone's passport saved offline
  • Travel insurance policy number shared in group chat
  • Live location sharing on for the whole crew
  • Copy of hostel addresses in each phone
Good to know

Quick safety tips

🌬️ Ferry delays

Summer "meltemi" winds occasionally cancel high-speed ferries — don't book your flight home for the same day as your last boat.

☀️ Sun & heat

June sun is intense — reef-safe sunscreen, a refillable water bottle, and a midday shade break go a long way.

🌊 Beach flags

Red flags mean don't swim. Rocky entries at spots like Red Beach can be slippery — water shoes help.
