What to Wear

The Greek-island uniform is easy: flowy whites, one crochet piece, gold shell jewelry, and a straw bag. Here's how to build each look — the sunset fit, the beach club day, and the night out — so every photo comes out effortless.

Look 1

Sunset & Dinner Fits

The whitewashed walls and blue domes do half the work — keep it flowy, white, and photo-ready.

The flowy white maxi dress

The undisputed Greece hero piece — a breezy white maxi against the caldera is the shot. Corset or smocked bodice, tiered skirt, straps that work over sunscreen shoulders.

Soft blue or pastel for the domes

A pale blue or butter-yellow dress pops beautifully against the blue-domed churches without clashing. Save this one for your Oia or Little Venice photo day.

Fitted cami + white maxi skirt

The internet's favorite formula: a slinky ribbed or lace cami tucked into a white tiered maxi skirt. Mix-and-match, packs flat, and reads "expensive" for almost nothing.

Look 2

Beach Club Day

Paradise Beach, Super Paradise, Scorpios — you're in a swimsuit from noon to sunset. Cover-ups are the whole outfit.

Swimsuit + crochet cover-up

A cream crochet skirt or cardigan over a bright bikini is the exact beach-club look — sunbed to beach-bar lunch with zero outfit change. Stack a few gold rings.

Sheer lace maxi cover-up

A long sheer white cover-up over a swimsuit reads effortlessly done-up and photographs dreamily in the shallows. Throw it on for the walk to the water taxi.

Crochet top + eyelet skirt

If you want out of the swimsuit for lunch, a knit crochet cami with a white eyelet or broderie skirt keeps the whole beachy palette going. Add slides you can kick off.

Look 3

Town & Nightlife

Athens rooftops and Mykonos clubs read "elevated resort," not hometown-club — think slip skirts, linen, and one statement piece.

Satin slip skirt or flowy dress

A satin slip skirt (the red or pink floral ones are everywhere right now) with a simple cami, or a flowy dress, carries you from a caldera dinner to a rooftop bar.

A little white / mini dress

One dressier mini for the Mykonos night out. Pair with strappy block-heel sandals — cobblestones will eat a stiletto alive, and you'll be dancing.

Linen wide-leg + a pretty top

White linen wide-leg trousers with a printed bandana-style or lace top is the easy, cooler-night alternative to a dress — and doubles as your travel-day outfit.

The details

Accessories that tie it all together

The clothes are simple on purpose — the accessories are what make it look styled.

🧺 The starter kit

A woven straw or raffia bag, oversized sunglasses, and a packable sun hat go with literally every look above — and cover you for sun at the same time.

✨ Finish every look with…

  • Gold shell / starfish jewelry layered necklaces
  • Stacked gold bangles + rings the "clean girl" detail
  • White H-strap or leather slides day & night
  • A block-heel sandal for nights out
  • A denim or linen layer for ferry-deck breeze
Pack smart

General packing tips

You're changing islands every 2 days. Pack mix-and-match pieces (2 bottoms, 4 tops) rather than full separate outfits — you'll do laundry or hand-wash at least once, and less luggage makes ferry transfers and stairs much easier.

Bring one modest cover-up. Some churches and monasteries (even casual ones you wander into in Plaka or Oia) expect shoulders and knees covered — a light scarf or long shirt solves this without a dedicated outfit.

Comfortable walking shoes are non-negotiable. The Acropolis, Ancient Agora, and Akrotiri all involve uneven ancient stone — save the going-out shoes for the going-out looks.

One tote bag, always. Beach-to-dinner, ferry snacks, wet swimsuit storage — a packable tote earns its weight more than almost anything else you bring.
