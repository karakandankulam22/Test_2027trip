Look good, stay comfortable

What to Wear

Three outfit moments worth planning ahead for — the Oia sunset photo, the beach club day, and the night out. Everything else, just pack light and versatile.

Look 1

The Oia Sunset Fit

You'll be standing on stairs and cobblestone for an hour waiting for golden hour — dress for that.

Flowy dress or linen set

A breezy maxi dress or matching linen co-ord photographs beautifully against the white-and-blue backdrop and moves well in the evening wind off the caldera.

Comfortable block sandals

Skip anything with a thin heel — Oia's paths are steep stone stairs. A block heel or nice flat sandal still looks great in photos.

Light layer for the wind

A thin cardigan or linen shirt over your shoulders — it gets breezy right as the sun drops, and it doubles as shoulder cover if you duck into a church.

Look 2

Beach Club Day

Paradise Beach, Super Paradise, Scorpios — you're in a swimsuit from noon to sunset.

Swimsuit + sheer cover-up

A crochet or sheer cover-up over your swimsuit works from the sunbed straight into a beach-bar lunch — no outfit change needed.

Oversized sunglasses + straw hat

Both do double duty as sun protection and the two easiest accessories to look put-together in every beach photo.

Slides, not sandals with buckles

You'll be in and out of them constantly for sand and water — slip-ons save you the hassle every single time.

Look 3

Nightlife / Going Out

Athens rooftop bars and Mykonos clubs read a little more "elevated resort" than club-in-your-hometown.

Slip dress or trousers + going-out top

Linen trousers or a slip dress with a nicer top works for both a rooftop bar in Athens and a beach club at night.

Cute shoes you can actually walk in

Cobblestones + a night of bar-hopping = save the stilettos. A block sandal or stylish sneaker will get much more wear.

Light jacket for the ferry-deck breeze

If your night out involves a boat or a late walk back along the water, a light jacket or denim layer earns its space in your bag.

Pack smart

General packing tips

You're changing islands every 2 days. Pack mix-and-match pieces (2 bottoms, 4 tops) rather than full separate outfits — you'll do laundry or hand-wash at least once, and less luggage makes ferry transfers and stairs much easier.

Bring one modest cover-up. Some churches and monasteries (even casual ones you wander into in Plaka or Oia) expect shoulders and knees covered — a light scarf or long shirt solves this without a dedicated outfit.

Comfortable walking shoes are non-negotiable. The Acropolis, Ancient Agora, and Akrotiri all involve uneven ancient stone — save the going-out shoes for the going-out looks.

One tote bag, always. Beach-to-dinner, ferry snacks, wet swimsuit storage — a packable tote earns its weight more than almost anything else you bring.
